About

The story
behind the work

Brand Strategy Social Media Science Writing Crisis Comms Content Strategy Digital Marketing Community Impact Philanthropy

Amanda Song is a digital media and brand strategist with a decade of experience at the intersection of science, education, and social impact. Her career began in the newsroom at NBC News MACH, where she wrote about NASA missions, emerging biotechnologies, and the frontiers of robotics — building a foundation in research-driven science and tech storytelling that still defines her work today.

That same rigor carried her into higher education, where she managed social media, web content, and institutional communications for the College of Liberal Arts & Sciences at UConn — and where she led digital engagement strategy during the COVID-19 crisis, ensuring communities received clear, consistent, and compassionate information when it mattered most.

Today, Amanda serves as Director of Digital & Community Investment Marketing at the mission-driven Federal Home Loan Bank San Francisco, where she leads all social, digital, and communications strategy around community investment grants, connecting mission-driven housing impact to the audiences it was built to serve.

Beyond her institutional work, Amanda has grown her own digital platforms to over 16 million views and 220,000+ followers — proof that she doesn't just understand brand storytelling in theory. She lives it.
